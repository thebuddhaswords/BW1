Saṁyutta Nikāya — The Connected Discourses

SN12: Connected Discourses on Causation

SN12:47 Jaṇussoṇi

  • © Translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Bodhi.  (More copyright information)

1At Sāvatthī. Then the brahmin Jaṇussoṇi approached the Blessed One and exchanged greetings with him. When they had concluded their greetings and cordial talk, he sat down to one side and said to him:[n.127] A brahmin Jaṇussoṇi is mentioned at SN45.4 and elsewhere in the Nikāyas. Spk says that he was a great chaplain (mahāpurohita) of much wealth who had gained his name by reason of his position. On the theme of this sutta see SN12.15.

2"How is it, Master Gotama: does all exist?" "‘All exists’: this, brahmin, is one extreme."

3"Then, Master Gotama, does all not exist?"

"‘All does not exist’: this, brahmin, is the second extreme. Without veering towards either of these extremes, the Tathāgata teaches the Dhamma by the middle … ."

4When this was said, the brahmin Jaṇussoṇi said to the Blessed One: sn.ii.77 "Magnificent, Master Gotama! … From today let Master Gotama remember me as a lay follower who has gone for refuge for life."

1Sāvatthiyaṁ vihārati. Atha kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavatā saddhiṁ … pe … ekamantaṁ nisinno kho jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṁ etadavoca: 

2"Kiṁ nu kho, bho gotama, sabbamatthī"ti? "‘Sabbamatthī’ti kho, brāhmaṇa, ayameko anto".

3"Kiṁ pana, bho gotama, sabbaṁ natthī"ti?

"‘Sabbaṁ natthī’ti kho, brāhmaṇa, ayaṁ dutiyo anto. Ete te, brāhmaṇa, ubho ante anupāgamma majjhena tathāgato dhammaṁ deseti: ‘avijjāpaccayā saṅkhārā; saṅkhārapaccayā viññāṇaṁ … pe … evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti. Avijjāya tveva asesavirāganirodhā saṅkhāranirodho; saṅkhāranirodhā viññāṇanirodho … pe … evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa nirodho hotī’"ti.

4Evaṁ vutte, jāṇussoṇi brāhmaṇo bhagavantaṁ etadavoca: "Abhikkantaṁ, bho gotama … pe … pāṇupetaṁ saraṇaṁ gatan"ti.

Sattamaṁ.
